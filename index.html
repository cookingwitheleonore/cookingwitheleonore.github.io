About Me


Hi! My name is Eléonore Cedroni and I've loved cooking for as long as I can remember. I can honestly say that there isn't much I enjoy more in life than cooking and eating. I am French and like most French people I think that the love of food runs through my veins. I grew up eating homemade meals and cooking with my mom and grandmother.

Before college I decided to attend culinary school in NYC in order to be able to cook anything my stomach desired. Fast forward 10 years: I graduated culinary school with a Chef's Diploma, did my cooking internship in a 2 star Michelin restaurant in Paris, went to college, graduated with Honors, worked in the food industry where I helped develop one of the most famous French pastry shops internationally as well as organizing cooking classes for children, and finally realized along the way that what I enjoyed most was cooking in my kitchen with young people who are passionate about food and getting their hands dirty. So if your kido loves food, loves to cook, wants to learn how to cook, you've come to the right place!

HOW IT WORKS



Location: My mother's kitchen on the Upper East Side (conveniantly located minutes away to most schools) or at your house. Number of children: Maximum 4. Age of the child: 10 to 18 years old. Price: $50 per child What does the price include: groceries, a recipee card to take home, and snack (eating what they've just cooked)! How long is a class: 1 hour.

WINTER 2017 CLASSES


Crêpes (french pancakes that you eat with jam, nutella, sugar & lemon...) Madeleines Toasts: sugar pea, mint & pecorino + avocado toast. Something else your child would like to make? Email me and we can figure something out: e.bebear@gmail.com

WINTER 2017 CLASSES

Madeleines
These shell-shaped butter cakes (that us French think of more as a cookie) make for a delicious dessert or snack. They are often enjoyed with tea but are also super tasty dipped in hot cocoa!
Crepes
A French specialty that resembles a very thin pancake. They can be dressed up with a number of sweet or savory fillings. Even though they're eaten all year round in France it is the custom to eat them on February 2nd for "La Chandeleur". {Option to use buckwheat flour to make them gluten free / almond milk to make them dairy free}.
Toasts
We will be making two recipes of toast on country bread which are great for a light lunch or cut up into small portions for parties. Avocado toast, which has become very popular and is one of my personal favorites. Green pea, pecorino & mint toast, which is sweet and refreshing. Both these toasts are simply fabulous!
